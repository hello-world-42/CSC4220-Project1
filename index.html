All about Dadar Gulung!

So, you wanna learn about dadar gulung, huh?

Dadar gulung (dah-dawr goo-loo-ng) is a traditional Indonesian recipe where pandan flavored crepes are filled with a sweet coconut filling. It's absolutely amazing and something that I've had the privilege of enjoying growing up. My mom makes dadar gulung about every couple months but always on special occasions too. I hope to spread this distinct sweetness to you as well!

Recipe

Crepes

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 and 3/4 cup coconut milk
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 tsp pandan flavoring
  • 1 egg
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • Oil for cooking

Filling

  • 2 cups shredded coconut
  • 1 cup chopped/grated palm sugar (sub with coconut sugar or brown sugar)
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1-2 drops pandan flavoring

To Cook the Filling

  1. In a medium saucepan, add all the filling ingredients and over low to medium low heat. Keep mixing until the palm sugar has melted and the coconut browns. Transfer to a bowl when done.

To Cook the Crepes

  1. In a large bowl, add the coconut milk, water, egg, pandan flavoring, salt, and flour. Whisk until all ingredients are incorporated together.
  2. Add a thin layer of oil to a pan on medium-high heat (very thin; use a brush or paper towel to spread a small amount of oil around the pan).
  3. Add an amount of crepe batter that spreads across the pan in a thin layer (ladle it in and move the pan in a circular motion to move the batter around). It shouldn't take very long (30 seconds to a minute) and the entire crepe should be set, not liquid at all. Move to a plate when done. Repeat for all your batter.
  4. Finally, in a laid out crepe, add a couple spoonfuls of the filling and wrap it up. That's it; enjoy!
